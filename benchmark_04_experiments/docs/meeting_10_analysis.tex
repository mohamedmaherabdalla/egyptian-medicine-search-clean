\begin{abstract}
This report consolidates the work completed from Meeting 10 through the latest
Algorithm 4 enhancement. It joins 595 OCR observations from 14 source models,
464 collision-free unique OCR query-target pairs, 10 retrieval systems, 24
Algorithm 4 configurations, eight equal-distance policies, and a deterministic
115,000-case synthetic benchmark. Algorithm 4 reaches 47.6293\% Hit@1 and
71.1207\% Hit@20 on the primary OCR denominator. It exceeds exhaustive
Levenshtein by 13.3621 percentage points at Hit@1 and Jaro-Winkler by 3.6638
points at Hit@20. Compact edit distance four or more creates 120 of 134 primary
top-20 misses. Removing family rescue lowers Hit@20 by 20.0431 points. On the
full synthetic benchmark, Algorithm 4 reaches 82.1165\% Hit@1, 93.4122\%
Hit@20, 93.6374\% behavior success, and zero unsafe confident first results.
Fair collision-excluded scoring raises all four synthetic algorithms by
3.4275--3.7529 Hit@1 points without deleting any source row. The pharmacist
study has 1,125 balanced assignments but no participant outcomes.
\end{abstract}

\section{Key Findings}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Algorithm 4 has the best tested OCR retrieval result: 47.6293\%
        Hit@1 and 71.1207\% Hit@20.} On the same 464 unique fair pairs, the
        strongest classical alternatives reach 34.2672\% Hit@1 and 67.4569\%
        Hit@20, so the gain is mostly first-rank ordering plus a smaller
        candidate-recall gain.
  \item \textbf{Four compact edits mark the practical retrieval break.}
        Hit@20 falls from 93.5484\% for two-to-three edits to 41.9355\% for
        four-to-five edits and 4.3478\% for six or more, which concentrates
        89.55\% of primary top-20 misses at distance four or above.
  \item \textbf{Family rescue is the dominant Algorithm 4 component.}
        Removing it reduces Hit@1 from 47.6293\% to 27.3707\% and Hit@20 from
        71.1207\% to 51.0776\%, with 96 gains versus two losses for complete
        Algorithm 4 at rank 1.
  \item \textbf{Safety cannot be inferred from retrieval accuracy.} Removing
        the clarification gate leaves Hit@1 and Hit@20 unchanged but raises
        unsafe confident first results from 0\% to 24.7845\%.
  \item \textbf{Algorithm 4 improves Algorithm 3 on the 115,000 paired cases.}
        Hit@1 rises by 1.0817 points and Hit@20 by 0.3174 points, with zero
        unsafe confident first results for both systems.
  \item \textbf{Fair scoring changes every synthetic algorithm, not only
        Algorithm 4.} Removing 5,026 exact-real-name collisions from the
        single-answer denominator raises A1--A4 Hit@1 by 3.4275, 3.6242,
        3.7025, and 3.7529 points; the 115,000 rows remain stored for safety
        diagnostics.
  \item \textbf{Human-study claims are not available.} The counterbalanced
        protocol contains 15 participants, 75 crops, and 1,125 assignments,
        but accuracy, decision time, and safety cannot be reported until real
        responses and adjudicated labels exist.
\end{itemize}

Compact form lowercases text and removes non-alphanumeric characters. For
\code{CLEX A NE} $\rightarrow$ \code{CLEXANE}, source alignment counts two
spaces as edits, while both compact strings are \code{clexane} and compact
distance is zero. With compact query $q_c$, target $y_c$, and Levenshtein
distance $L$,
\[
d=L(q_c,y_c), \qquad r=\frac{d}{\max(|y_c|,1)}.
\]
An extreme prediction has $r>0.60$. For \code{GHONT} $\rightarrow$
\code{CALAMINE}, $d=7$, $|y_c|=8$, and $r=0.875$. The 0.60 threshold is an
operational review band, not a probability or clinical-risk estimate.

\subsection{Metrics and Statistical Tests}

For query $q$, $R_k(q)$ is the first $k$ returned families and $Y(q)$ is the
verified relevant-family set:
\[
\operatorname{Hit@k}(q)=\mathbf{1}\!\left[R_k(q)\cap Y(q)\neq\emptyset\right].
\]

\begin{itemize}
  \item Hit@1 failure with Hit@20 success is a ranking failure; Hit@20 failure
        is a candidate-retrieval failure.
  \item MRR@20 uses reciprocal rank for the first relevant result through rank
        20 and zero beyond rank 20.
  \item Paired risk differences use 10,000 bootstrap resamples with seed
        20260719. Error bars are 95\% percentile intervals.
  \item Exact two-sided McNemar tests use pairwise gains and losses. Matched odds
        ratios describe effect direction. Exploratory p-values are not adjusted
        for multiple comparisons and are not treated as confirmatory evidence.
  \item Spearman correlation measures monotonic association. It does not assign
        causality to edit distance, evidence counts, or recovery.
\end{itemize}

\section{Failures and Dead Ends}

These findings identify work that failed, remains unsupported, or would produce
misleading claims if reported as success.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Extreme lexical corruption remains unsolved.} Algorithm 4 misses
        111 of 121 extreme observations by rank 20. (The cohort averages 6.16
        compact edits and cannot be repaired reliably by small score changes.)
  \item \textbf{Autocorrect artifacts contain insufficient signal.} Algorithm 4
        reaches 0\% Hit@1 and 3.65\% Hit@20. (Mappings such as a medicine to an
        unrelated English word require image evidence, a language model with
        constraints, or human clarification, not ordinary fuzzy matching.)
  \item \textbf{Keyboard-shift results are feature-specific.} Algorithm 4 reaches
        0.8\% Hit@1 and 2.0\% Hit@20. (Algorithms 1 and 3 score much higher
        because they contain a whole-keyboard shift reversal; the category tests
        that decoder, not general spelling robustness.)
  \item \textbf{Generic equal-distance rules did not transfer.} Edge-first
        improved development by 0.54 points but reduced holdout by 3.23 points.
        (No policy replaced current ordering.)
  \item \textbf{Several ablation components remain unproven on 464 pairs.}
        Removing suffix evidence or first-character expansion causes no switches;
        removing the external retriever or context cleanup slightly raises
        Hit@20. (Zero or favorable removal effects are evidence for review, not
        proof that a component is universally useless.)
  \item \textbf{Raw OCR-model ranking is confounded.} Models did not process a
        matched image set. (Case severity, target distribution, and row count
        differ by model.)
  \item \textbf{DrugEye has no matched human-study outcome.} Historical web
        probes and cached sample results do not replace randomized participant
        decisions. (No clinical or workflow superiority claim is made.)
\end{itemize}

\section{Open Questions and Next Steps}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Audit repeated extreme OCR pairs against original crops, starting with
        exact alternative real names such as \code{INDOMETHACIN} and
        \code{NEURONTIN}; freeze corrected labels before further tuning.
  \item Add bounded operation-aware candidate generation for four-to-five-edit
        rows with at least one shared bigram; evaluate on development, then
        require a holdout Hit@20 gain without increasing unsafe confidence.
  \item Rerank ranks 2--5 using family-head, weighted edit, and agreement
        evidence; 89 of the 140 inclusive rank-only cases are resolved by rank 5.
  \item Redesign autocorrect, fragmentation, doctor abbreviation, and keyboard
        shift categories so scoring reflects recoverable evidence and valid
        ambiguity rather than one forced expected family.
  \item Run all 14 OCR systems on the same de-identified crop set with one
        preprocessing contract, then report exact OCR accuracy and downstream
        Algorithm 4 recovery separately.
  \item Execute the pharmacist study only after two independent pharmacists
        adjudicate every crop; report correct selection, safe deferral,
        unsafe wrong selection, decision time, and confidence with participant-
        and case-clustered uncertainty.
\end{enumerate}
